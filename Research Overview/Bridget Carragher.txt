Researcher: Bridget Carragher
Research Overview:
Bridget Carragher's research focuses on the forefront of structural biology, particularly utilizing advanced techniques in cryo-electron microscopy (cryo-EM) to investigate various viral and protein structures, including those associated with Zika virus, dengue, and SARS-CoV-2. Through her work, she emphasizes the importance of automation and machine learning in improving the resolution and efficiency of single-particle analysis and sample preparation methods. Carragher's studies encompass a broad array of topics, such as vaccine design, protein-protein interactions, liposome development, and the elucidation of complex biological mechanisms like receptor activation and mechanotransduction. By integrating computational advancements with experimental techniques, her research aims to democratize access to high-resolution structural analyses and facilitate the discovery and design of therapeutics, vaccines, and diagnostic tools.
