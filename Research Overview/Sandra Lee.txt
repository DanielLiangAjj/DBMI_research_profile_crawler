Researcher: Sandra Lee
Research Overview:
Sandra Lee's research focuses on the intersection of genetics, ethics, and health equity, addressing the implications of genetic therapies and precision medicine among diverse and marginalized populations. Her work emphasizes the need for inclusive practices in genetic counseling, research participation, and result dissemination, particularly regarding hereditary cancer syndromes and rare genetic diseases. By incorporating principles of bioethics and community engagement, her studies advocate for equitable access to genetic resources and information while navigating the ethical complexities associated with genetic testing and counseling. Additionally, her interdisciplinary approach highlights the importance of team science and collaboration across various fields, aiming to enhance the understanding of genetic ancestry and its implications on health disparities. Overall, Lee's research aims to foster greater inclusivity and justice within biomedical research, promoting health equity through informed genetic practices and broader stakeholder engagement.
