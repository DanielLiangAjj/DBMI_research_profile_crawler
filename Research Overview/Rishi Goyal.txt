Researcher: Rishi Goyal
Research Overview:
Rishi Goyal's research primarily focuses on the intersection of community health, patient-centered care, and clinical decision-making in the context of urban healthcare environments, especially in New York City. His studies emphasize the roles of community health workers in enhancing trust and communication between healthcare providers and diverse patient populations, including older adults. By employing qualitative research methodologies, Goyal explores innovative approaches to healthcare delivery, particularly in addressing community-acquired infections like pneumonia and cancer-related comorbidities. His work delves into the psychological models of cooperative behavior and decision-making, advocating for evidence-based and integrative medicine practices that foster patient participation and improve health outcomes. This research is critical in understanding the dynamics of physician-patient relations and the effectiveness of health interventions across varied demographics, contributing to the overall advancement of community health initiatives.
