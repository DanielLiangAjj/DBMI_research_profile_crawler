Researcher: Sarah Collins Rossetti
Research Overview:
Sarah Collins Rossetti's research focuses on the intersection of health informatics, particularly the application of electronic health records (EHRs) and advanced machine learning techniques, to enhance patient outcomes in heart failure management and home care services. By leveraging natural language processing and predictive modeling, her work aims to identify clinical deterioration and facilitate risk assessment, thereby improving clinical decision support systems for nurses and physicians. The integration of nursing informatics emphasizes standardized documentation and workflow optimization, crucial for reducing hospitalization rates and improving patient safety. Rossetti also explores qualitative methodologies to understand professional burnout and the engagement of healthcare providers, underscoring the importance of interdisciplinary collaboration and effective communication within healthcare systems. Overall, her research aims to drive quality improvement in nursing care and health services through innovative technological solutions and participatory approaches, fostering a patient-centered care environment.
