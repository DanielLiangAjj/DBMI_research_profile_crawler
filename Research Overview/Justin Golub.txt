Researcher: Justin Golub
Research Overview:
Dr. Justin Golub's research predominantly focuses on the interconnections between hearing loss, cognitive dysfunction, and mental health issues, particularly in aging populations. His studies explore various aspects such as the prevalence of hearing loss among older adults, the implications of hearing impairment on depression and cognitive decline, and the effectiveness of hearing aids as a treatment modality. Golub emphasizes the influence of socioeconomic status and demographics, including racial disparities, on both hearing health and mental well-being. Additionally, his work examines the outcomes of otologic surgical procedures, highlighting quality of life and health outcomes. Overall, Dr. Golub's research contributes significantly to understanding how auditory health intersects with psychological and cognitive factors, advocating for improved access and personalized approaches to auditory care in vulnerable populations.
