Researcher: Hashim M. Al-Hashimi
Research Overview:
Hashim M. Al-Hashimi's research focuses on the intricate dynamics of nucleic acids, emphasizing DNA and RNA structures, their recognition mechanisms, and the resulting implications for mutation, replication, and repair processes. His work extensively investigates DNA damage, including the roles of oxidative stress markers like 8-oxoguanine, and explores the influence of conformational dynamics and excited state ensembles on mutational signatures. Al-Hashimi employs advanced techniques such as solution-state NMR and molecular dynamics simulations to elucidate the structural characteristics of nucleic acids, unveiling critical insights into processes like RNA folding, drug discovery in HIV, and interactions involving specific response elements. His interdisciplinary approach bridges structural biology with computational methods, enabling a deeper understanding of biological computation related to drug actions and epigenetic modifications. Overall, Al-Hashimi's research contributes significantly to unraveling the complexities of nucleic acid behavior and its broader biological implications.
