Researcher: Anum Glasgow
Research Overview:
Anum Glasgow's research focuses on the intricate field of biomolecular interactions, emphasizing computational drug design and the dynamics of protein-ligand and protein-protein interactions. Notably, her work addresses critical health challenges, including the development of antiviral therapeutics against SARS-CoV-2, leveraging techniques like molecular docking simulations and yeast display to engineer proteins with enhanced binding properties. Her investigations extend to the virulence mechanisms of pathogens such as Salmonella and Escherichia coli, utilizing concepts like type III secretion systems and gene networks. Additionally, her research explores innovative applications such as microbial electrocatalysis and biomineralization, highlighting the versatility of protein engineering and nanotechnology. Through employing a variety of methodologies, including X-ray crystallography and dynamic light scattering, Glasgow aims to elucidate the fundamental principles governing biomolecular interactions, fostering advancements in drug design and therapeutic interventions.
