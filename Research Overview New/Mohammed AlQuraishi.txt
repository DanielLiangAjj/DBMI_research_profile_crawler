Researcher: Mohammed AlQuraishi
Research Overview:
Mohammed AlQuraishi's research encompasses a broad spectrum of topics primarily centered on computational biology, protein structure prediction, and the application of machine learning and deep learning techniques in these domains. His work notably includes the exploration of protein folding, design, and interactions, with specific emphasis on utilizing advanced algorithms such as AlphaFold2 for accurate protein structure prediction. Additionally, AlQuraishi investigates the implications of metabolic diseases, malnutrition, and nutritional status on health, particularly within adult and geriatric populations. His studies leverage questionnaires for validation, examining behaviors and chronic conditions, including stress and depression, while employing computational models to analyze protein dynamics and mutations. Integration of deep learning approaches also extends to cancer metabolism, where he assesses the role of long non-coding RNAs, apoptosis, and autophagy in cellular processes. Overall, AlQuraishi's research contributes significant insights into the molecular mechanisms underlying health and disease, utilizing sophisticated computational methodologies to bridge gaps across biological and clinical disciplines.
