Researcher: Clinton Potter
Research Overview:
Clinton Potter's research focuses on the intersection of automation and structural biology, particularly through the utilization of cryogenic electron microscopy (cryoEM). His work emphasizes advancements in biophysics education and the training necessary for effective data collection and management in high-throughput environments. Potter explores the intricacies of structural data related to protein nanoparticles, virus-like particles, and other complex biomolecules, enhancing methods in structure-based drug discovery. His investigations also delve into methodologies for data archiving, image processing, and the optimization of workflows in cryoEM, ensuring reproducibility and efficiency in scientific research. Through the integration of robotics and computational techniques, his contributions aim to streamline the analysis of molecular structures, offering valuable insights into molecular docking simulations and protein binding dynamics, thus reinforcing the foundations of drug design and discovery processes.
