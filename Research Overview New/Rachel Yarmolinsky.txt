Researcher: Rachel Yarmolinsky
Research Overview:
Rachel Yarmolinsky's research primarily focuses on the ethical implications and biases associated with genomic research and its applications. By examining issues of equity and justice within the context of genetics, her work addresses critical bioethical questions that arise in clinical settings. Yarmolinsky engages with a variety of stakeholders, including ethicists and ethics committees, to explore how ethical frameworks can guide equitable practices in genomics. Her findings contribute to a deeper understanding of the societal impact of genetic technologies and emphasize the importance of ethics in shaping policies that govern their use, particularly in the United States. Overall, her research advocates for a balanced approach that prioritizes ethical considerations while promoting fairness and social responsibility within the field of genetics.
