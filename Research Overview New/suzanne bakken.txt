Researcher: suzanne bakken
Research Overview:
Suzanne Bakken's research significantly revolves around the intersection of artificial intelligence, nursing education, and informatics, emphasizing the enhancement of healthcare delivery through technology and data science. Her work encompasses a diverse array of topics including nursing informatics, graduate nursing education, and health equity, with a particular focus on vulnerable populations such as Hispanic and Latino communities. Bakken's studies explore the application of generative AI, natural language processing, and information visualization techniques to improve health literacy and patient engagement, particularly for chronic disease management and mental health. By employing methods like literature analysis, scoping reviews, and participatory design, she aims to identify and address barriers within healthcare systems, advocating for better access to health resources and data-driven decision-making. Her contributions advance not only nursing practice and education but also aim to foster equity in health outcomes through innovative uses of informatics and technology in patient care.
