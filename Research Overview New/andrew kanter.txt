Researcher: andrew kanter
Research Overview:
Andrew Kanter's research focuses on the intersection of technology and healthcare in sub-Saharan African countries, particularly Ghana and Sierra Leone. His work emphasizes the utilization of mobile health (mHealth) and telemedicine solutions to enhance healthcare delivery, midwifery practices, and patient outcomes in underserved regions. The use of electronic health information systems and mobile devices is a key theme, as Kanter investigates methods for improving the usability and effectiveness of these tools within local healthcare settings. His research also addresses critical areas such as HIV infections, child nutrition disorders, and chronic disease management, highlighting the importance of capacity building, community health services, and the role of health personnel in implementing these technologies. By analyzing the impact and efficiency of interventions, Kanter contributes to the global health discourse, advocating for innovative healthcare solutions tailored to the unique challenges faced by developing countries in Africa.
